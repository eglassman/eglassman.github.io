Current research interests: human-computer interaction, learning at scale, and computer science education
  • MIT CSAIL I am an EECS Ph.D. candidate in the User Interface Design group advised by Rob Miller.
  • Google This summer I'm working with Dan Russell on designing interfaces that help users learn.
  • Microsoft Research Last summer, I worked on a system for translating the MuddyCard technique to online lecture videos with Meredith Ringel Morris and Andrés Monroy-Hernández.
  • Jerusalem I was an instructor and am now the Student President of MIT-MEET, which teaches gifted Israelis and Palestinians computer science and teamwork in Jerusalem.


I completed my M.Eng. in the MIT CSAIL Robot Locomotion Group and received the MIT EECS Dept. Masterworks Oral Thesis Presentation Award. I also spent a semester as a visiting researcher at the Stanford Biomimetics and Dexterous Manipulation Lab working on control systems for birdlike flying robots.

Prior to my time in robotics, I published work in biomedical signal processing with the mentorship of John Guttag at MIT CSAIL and Jack Gelfand at Princeton University.

My graduate research was funded by the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship, the National Defense Science and Engineering Graduate Fellowship (NDSEG), and the MIT Amar Bose Teaching Fellowship. My undergraduate degree was funded in part by the Intel Foundation Young Scientist Award ($50,000) given to the top 3 out of 1300 projects at the 2003 Intel International Science and Engineering Fair and the IEEE President’s Scholarship. I have also been inducted into the National Gallery for America’s Young Inventors.
