I am an EECS Ph.D. candidate at MIT CSAIL advised by Rob Miller. My current research interests are human-computer interaction, learning at scale, and computer science education. I worked on a system for translating the MuddyCard technique to online lecture videos with Meredith Ringel Morris and Andrés Monroy-Hernández at Microsoft Research last summer. This summer I'm working with Dan Russell at Google on designing interfaces that help users learn. I completed my M.Eng. in the MIT CSAIL Robot Locomotion Group. I also spent a semester as a visiting researcher at the Stanford Biomimetics and Dexterous Manipulation Lab working on control systems for birdlike flying robots. Prior to my time in robotics, I published work in biomedical signal processing with the mentorship of John Guttag and Jack Gelfand. I am also currently the Student President of MIT-MEET, which teaches gifted Israelis and Palestinians computer science and teamwork in Jerusalem.
